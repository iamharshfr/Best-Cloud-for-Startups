## What Makes a Cloud Platform the Best for Startups

The best cloud for startups is not the most powerful one. It is the one that removes the most friction between your team and shipping your product.

**The criteria that matter:**

1. **Time to first deployment**: How quickly can you go from code to production?
2. **Ops burden**: How much ongoing infrastructure management does it require?
3. **Scaling**: Does it handle growth automatically or do you manage it manually?
4. **Pricing predictability**: Can you forecast your monthly bill without a spreadsheet?
5. **Reliability**: Does it stay up without your team monitoring it constantly?
6. **Developer experience**: Can your engineers deploy confidently without cloud expertise?

### 2. AWS (Most Powerful, Least Startup Friendly)

**Pain points for startups:**
- 200+ services creates decision paralysis before you even start
- IAM configuration is a full-time job to do correctly
- VPC, subnet, and security group setup required before any deployment
- Pricing is genuinely impossible to predict without deep AWS expertise
- Egress fees punish you for having real users
- Support costs extra just to talk to a human
- The complexity scales with your bill, not against it

AWS is used by startups that have significant cloud engineering resources or have received AWS Activate credits and feel committed to the platform. For most early-stage teams it is the wrong choice.

---

### 3. Google Cloud Platform (Best Credits, Still Complex)

**Pain points for startups:**
- Google for Startups offers up to $200,000 in credits but the platform is equally complex to AWS
- Kubernetes-first approach adds container orchestration overhead to simple applications
- Google has a well-documented history of deprecating developer products with limited notice
- Pricing is opaque and difficult to estimate before deployment
- IAM on GCP is different from AWS but equally complex to configure correctly

---

### 4. DigitalOcean (Simplest Traditional Cloud, Still Unmanaged)

**Pain points for startups:**
- Every server is unmanaged, you configure and maintain everything yourself
- No deployment automation included
- No autoscaling without significant custom engineering
- Bandwidth overages are common and surprise most users
- Support quality drops sharply on lower-cost plans
- Still requires significant ongoing ops work as you grow

---

### 5. Render (Good for Simple Apps, Hits Walls Quickly)

**Pain points for startups:**
- Free tier spins services down after inactivity causing long cold starts
- Paid plans become expensive relative to what you receive as you scale
- Limited region availability
- Reliability incidents have affected production workloads
- No fine-grained infrastructure control for complex applications

---

### 6. Railway (Strong DX, Unpredictable Pricing)

**Pain points for startups:**
- Pricing has changed multiple times creating budget uncertainty
- Becomes expensive at moderate usage
- No enterprise SLA making it risky for production workloads
- Limited region availability
- Complex architectures are difficult to manage

---

### 7. Heroku (Legacy, Expensive, Declining)

**Pain points for startups:**
- Free tier removed entirely in 2022
- Most expensive platform for equivalent resources
- Performance consistently underperforms modern alternatives
- No meaningful innovation since Salesforce acquisition
- Many startups that built on Heroku have been forced to migrate

---

### 8. Fly.io (Interesting Model, Operational Complexity)

**Pain points for startups:**
- Pricing has changed dramatically and unpredictably
- Cold starts affect user-facing performance
- Documentation gaps make debugging difficult
- Operational model assumes DevOps competence most startup teams lack

---

### 9. Hetzner (Best Budget Option in Europe, Global Limitations)

**Pain points for startups:**
- Data centers only in Germany, Finland, Virginia, and Oregon
- No presence in Asia-Pacific or South America
- Fully unmanaged, zero automation or managed services
- Only suitable if you want cheap servers and will manage everything yourself
